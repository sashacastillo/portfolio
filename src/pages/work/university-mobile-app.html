Client

A public university, serving 32,000+ students.

Project Outcomes

  • Improved user experience on re-designed sections of the mobile app
  • Improved navigation and made high-usage features more accessible
  • Greater understanding of the user audience
  • Created a usability strategy and a roadmap for future changes

My Role

My role was growing the mobile app in adding new offerings, usability strategy, re-design, usability research and planning, adaptation/increasing usage of the app.

Usability Strategy and Future Roadmap

The main usability strategy goal for the mobile app was to continue to add value with new offerings and improve and iterate on current designs/information architecture. I created a rough roadmap covering the next two years.

Usability Testing: Interviews

I needed to display book stacks without overwhelming the users with too much detail. I opted out for providing multiple visual clues to help users locate books:

  • I divided the stack areas into visual sections.
  • Each stack location is accurately marked.
  • I added book call numbers to the sections.
  • This design was informed by how other libraries.

Navigation Studies

I conducted Cardsorting and Tree Testing Studies studies. The design changes resulted in increased usage of the app during the freshman orientation and we are now getting a lot more traffic directed at Athletics, My Class Map, and Events screens. Study results:

  • Increased traffic to highly used features
  • Improved navigation and categorization of content
  • Improved experience on the main screens

Foundation for Personalization of the App

In the old map design did not label book stacks, office areas inaccessible to patrons, group study rooms, and amenities. The team collectively worked out the visual design of the new map to solve these problems. I worked on the amenities icons, office areas, legend, 2nd floor stacks, and the overall visual style.

Lessons Learned

We used a Modo app building product that has a lot of limitations when it comes to layout and functionality. I learned that it is possible to be creative within limitations and still be able to improve the usefulness, usability, and aesthetics of the product.
